Doomsday Trail

The project

Completed July 2018. I attend a tech training Bootcamp for Front End Development. The program was 10 weeks and the last 2 weeks was spent on group final projects. My group made this game; ‘Doomsday Trail’. The premise of the game is that the power grid went out and the character is trying to make it to their friends hide out bunker. There is chaos in the streets but the character runs through 3 different stops and needs to collect as many calories in food as he or she can. The caveats: player can only hold 6 items in their bag at a time and player has a limited amount of time at each stop. We created our own algorithm to calculate how many calories the player needs per day based on their statistic they entered in and then at the end of the game we total up the amount of calories collected and let the user know how many days they can survive based on their caloric needs versus the amount they collected.

Focus

My focus on this project was finding an API that we could use to pull in nutritional information on the food, UX/UI for desktop and mobile and creating HTML/CSS that would give tha applicationa game feel.

What I learned

This was a great project to be involved with from start to finish. I learned best ways to communicate with different team members and how to successfully divide up tasks to meet deadlines. On the developing side I learned how to create and use sprite sheets for our characters that move in the game and also how to change up the layout from desktop to mobile with the user in mind.
